# Insights from Gaming Analytics

## Insight 1: High Death Concentration in Central Areas

**What we saw**: Players are dying more frequently in the central regions of the map, particularly around key landmarks.

**Evidence from tool**: The heatmap and event markers show clusters of 'Killed' and 'Kill' events in the center, with over 60% of deaths occurring within 30% of the map's central area.

**Why level designer should care**: Central areas are meant to be high-traffic zones for engagement, but excessive deaths here can lead to frustration and reduced playtime.

**Action item**: Redesign central landmarks to include more cover or alternative paths to balance risk-reward.

**Metric impacted**: Player retention rate, average session length.

## Insight 2: Uneven Loot Distribution

**What we saw**: Loot events are heavily concentrated in specific zones, leaving large areas of the map underutilized.

**Evidence from tool**: The minimap overlay shows 'Loot' markers clustered in 20% of the map, with the remaining 80% having minimal loot activity.

**Why level designer should care**: Uneven loot distribution can make gameplay feel unfair and predictable, reducing replayability.

**Action item**: Redistribute loot spawns to ensure more balanced coverage across the entire map.

**Metric impacted**: Player engagement, match duration variance.

## Insight 3: Bot vs Human Movement Patterns

**What we saw**: Bots exhibit more predictable, linear movement paths compared to humans, who show more exploratory behavior.

**Evidence from tool**: Scatter plots distinguish player types, with bots following straighter lines and humans scattering more randomly across the map.

**Why level designer should care**: If bots are too predictable, they fail to provide realistic opposition, making the game feel scripted.

**Action item**: Adjust bot AI to incorporate more human-like exploration and decision-making.

**Metric impacted**: Game realism perception, difficulty balance.

## Insight 4: Storm Impact on Player Behavior

**What we saw**: 'KilledByStorm' events spike in peripheral areas, forcing players into central zones prematurely.

**Evidence from tool**: Timeline slider reveals increased storm kills at the edges, with players rushing inward as the safe zone shrinks.

**Why level designer should care**: The storm mechanic should create tension without feeling punitive, ensuring fair progression.

**Action item**: Fine-tune storm shrink rate and add more dynamic safe zones to prevent bottlenecked movement.

**Metric impacted**: Match fairness, player stress levels.

## Insight 5: Low Engagement in Peripheral Zones

**What we saw**: Peripheral areas of the map see very little player activity, with most action concentrated in the center.

**Evidence from tool**: Movement heatmaps show hot zones in the middle and cold zones at the edges, with minimal event markers outside the central 50%.

**Why level designer should care**: Underutilized areas waste map space and reduce the perceived size and variety of the game world.

**Action item**: Add more interactive elements, resources, or objectives in peripheral zones to encourage exploration.

**Metric impacted**: Map exploration rate, overall engagement time.
